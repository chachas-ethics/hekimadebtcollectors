Privacy Policy

We are committed to protecting the privacy of all our clients, partners, debtors, and any individuals whose data we handle during the debt collection process. This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, store, use, and protect personal information in accordance with the laws of the United Republic of Tanzania and internationally accepted standards.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of data:

  • Client identity details (name, company name, contact numbers, TIN, address).
  • Debtor information provided legally by clients (name, phone number, address, email, ID numbers).
  • Financial details related to outstanding debts and payment agreements.
  • Communication records, such as calls, SMS, emails, and WhatsApp messages.
  • Information from public databases, legal documents, and regulatory records.

2. How We Use Personal Information

Personal data is used strictly for legitimate purposes, including:

  • Debt verification and identity confirmation.
  • Conducting lawful collection activities.
  • Documenting payment agreements and resolutions.
  • Client reporting and accountability.
  • Legal compliance and regulatory obligations.

3. Data Sharing

We do NOT share personal data with third parties unless:

  • We have written consent from the client or individual.
  • The sharing is required by law, court order, or governmental authority.
  • It is necessary for legal enforcement or verification.
We never sell personal information under any circumstances.

4. Data Protection & Security

We implement strong security controls such as encryption, secure digital storage, password-protected systems, and role-based access to ensure data confidentiality. Only authorized personnel can access sensitive information.

5. Data Retention

We retain records only as long as required by law or necessary to fulfill collection obligations. After this, data is securely deleted or archived according to our retention policy.

Regulatory Disclaimers

We operate in compliance with Tanzanian laws governing financial transactions, debt collection practices, data protection, and client rights. Where applicable, we also consider international laws for clients with operations outside Tanzania.

1. Compliance With Tanzanian Regulations

Our operations align with:

  • The Bank of Tanzania (BoT) financial regulations.
  • The Cybercrimes Act.
  • The Data Protection Act (DPA).
  • The Fair Competition Act (FCA).
  • Contract Law under the Laws of Tanzania.

2. Ethical Collection Practices

We do not:

  • Harass, threaten, or intimidate debtors.

  • Some countries require documentation such as notarized contracts, identity confirmations, and financial proof.
